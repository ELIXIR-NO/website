Human genomics in Europe is shifting from a research-led field to one funded through healthcare, driven by initiatives such as the 19-country commitment to sequence and share at least 1 million genomes. While some healthcare-generated genomic data will be available for secondary research, national legal restrictions often limit cross-border sharing.
The ELIXIR Federated Human Data Community aims to establish a secure, interoperable, and FAIR-compliant ecosystem enabling population-scale genomic and biomolecular data to be accessed across Europe. This three-year project will deliver metadata standards, interfaces, and a reference implementation within the European Genome-phenome Archive (EGA) framework, along with technical, logistical, and training support for ELIXIR Nodes to join the federated EGA network. Building on prior projects (ELIXIR-EXCELERATE, CORBEL, Tryggve), it will be led by EGA and include the development of a “Local EGA” software for operating federated nodes in line with governance and legal frameworks.
